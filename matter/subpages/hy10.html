HY10 - Buoys

Floating devices that rely on bouyancy to stay afloat.

Buoys are designed to float due to their shape and the materials they are made from, which are typically lighter than water. They displace a volume of water equal to their own weight, creating an upward buoyant force that counteracts gravity. This principle allows them to stay afloat on the water's surface.
