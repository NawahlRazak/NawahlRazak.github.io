S7 - Galton's Whistle and Sensitive Flame

A high-frequency whistle invented by Sir Francis Galton in 1876 to test the upper limits of human hearing and to study animal hearing

1) Galton's Whistle. Blow by means of rubber tube connected to gas tap. 2) Sensitive flame: turn yup gas pressure until flame is just about to flare. 3) Refraction of sound. Hold balloon filled with CO2 between whistle and sensitive flame. 4) Sensitive flame also responds to jingle of keys and the letter "S"
