H49 - Bunsen Ice Calorimeter

a device that uses the melting of ice to measure heat

It works by observing the volume change of a water-ice mixture as heat is added or removed, causing a corresponding change in the amount of mercury in the system. This change in mercury volume is directly related to the amount of heat transferred, allowing for the determination of heat capacity or other heat-related properties.
