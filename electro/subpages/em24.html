EM23 - Charge On Concentric Shells

Shows the distribution of charge on irregularly shaped objects.

Use the tapered brass cone that has one end pointed and the other, large end, rounded. Charge it by flicking it with flannel (or rubbing it with rabbit skin). Use a proof plane to remove charge from one end or the other, and transfer it to a gold leaf electroscope. Show that the charges are more concentrated at the pointed end. A small wooden cone on a stand can also be used for this demonstration.
