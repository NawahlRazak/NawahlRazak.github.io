EM2 - Audio Oscillator

Different circuits and components are used to create different waveform shapes (sine, square, triangle, etc.).

Works by generating a repeating electronic signal, or waveform, within the audible frequency range (20Hz to 20kHz). It achieves this by converting a direct current (DC) signal into an alternating current (AC) signal through a feedback loop that sustains oscillations. The specific waveform (sine, square, triangle, etc.) and frequency can be adjusted, making them useful for creating sounds in music production and other applications.
