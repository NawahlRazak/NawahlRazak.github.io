M172 - Driven Oscillations

Demonstration of resonance using a driven oscillator.

Resonance in a driven pendulum occurs when the frequency of the external driving force matches the pendulum's natural frequency, causing the pendulum to oscillate with a large amplitude. This phenomenon, known as resonance, is characterized by a maximum transfer of energy from the driving force to the pendulum, resulting in a significant increase in the pendulum's oscillation amplitude.
